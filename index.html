Dilek Düştegör

Hi there, Dilek Düştegör here!

I am an Assistant Professor with Education Profile at the Bernoulli Institute for Mathematics, Computer Science and Artificial Intelligence of the University of Groningen, Netherlands. I am a member of the Distributed Systems group. My current research is focused on distributed decision making for smart environments, cyber-physical systems and digital twins, and pervasive computing for ambient intelligence, with a particular interest in sustainable applications for critical systems like water and energy management.

As an engineer (both in training and at heart), I have always been passionate about practical applications of theoretical knowledge to solve real-world problems and improve existing technologies. My research interests span multiple areas at the intersection of computer science, distributed systems, and artificial intelligence. From an application perspective, I focus on designing intelligent, data-driven solutions for monitoring and managing large-scale networked infrastructures such as water distribution networks, energy systems, and industrial IoT environments. I am particularly interested in leveraging distributed digital twins, multi-agent systems, and collaborative AI to enable real-time decision-making and coordination among diverse stakeholders. From a theoretical perspective, my work touches on distributed intelligence, autonomous system coordination, scalability and fault tolerance in complex networks, and the integration of AI with domain-specific simulation models. I also explore the application of learning analytics, generative AI, and human–AI interaction to enhance educational feedback systems and assessment processes. Across both domains, my goal is to combine applied research with societal impact, creating solutions that are rigorous, practical, and aligned with the needs of the evolving digital world.
